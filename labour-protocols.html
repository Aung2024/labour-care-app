Labour Stages Overview

First Stage

Onset: Regular contractions + cervical changes

End: Full cervical dilation (10cm)

Duration:

  • Nulliparous: 12-20 hours
  • Multiparous: 6-12 hours
Second Stage

Onset: Full dilation + urge to push

End: Birth of baby

Duration:

  • Nulliparous: 1-3 hours
  • Multiparous: 0.5-2 hours
Third Stage

Onset: Birth of baby

End: Delivery of placenta

Duration:

  • Normal: 5-30 minutes
  • Alert: >30 minutes

Action Lines & Alert Thresholds

Alert Line (First Stage)
  • Cervical dilation rate <1cm/hour
  • Prolonged labour >12 hours (nulliparous)
  • Prolonged labour >8 hours (multiparous)
  • Fetal heart rate abnormalities
  • Maternal exhaustion
Action Line (Second Stage)
  • No progress for 1 hour (nulliparous)
  • No progress for 30 min (multiparous)
  • Fetal distress signs
  • Maternal distress
  • Consider assisted delivery

Emergency Interventions

When to Transfer/Refer
  • Prolonged labour beyond action line
  • Fetal distress (FHR <110 or >160 bpm)
  • Cord prolapse
  • Antepartum hemorrhage
  • Severe pre-eclampsia/eclampsia
  • Obstructed labour
  • Malpresentation (breech, transverse)
