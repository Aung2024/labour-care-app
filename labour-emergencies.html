Critical Labour Emergencies

IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED

These conditions require immediate intervention and possible transfer

Cord Prolapse
  1. 1
    Position mother
    Knee-chest or Trendelenburg position
  2. 2
    Reduce pressure
    Push presenting part up to relieve cord compression
  3. 3
    Immediate transfer
    Emergency cesarean section required
Shoulder Dystocia
  1. 1
    Call for help
    Alert additional staff immediately
  2. 2
    McRoberts position
    Flex hips, knees to chest
  3. 3
    Suprapubic pressure
    Apply downward pressure above pubic bone

Fetal Distress Management

Fetal Heart Rate Abnormalities

Normal FHR: 110-160 beats per minute

Bradycardia: <110 bpm for >10 minutes

Tachycardia: >160 bpm for >10 minutes

Immediate Actions for Fetal Distress
  1. 1
    Change maternal position
    Left lateral position to improve placental blood flow
  2. 2
    Administer oxygen
    Give oxygen at 8-10 L/min via face mask
  3. 3
    IV fluids
    Correct maternal hypotension if present
  4. 4
    Prepare for delivery
    Expedite delivery - consider assisted delivery or cesarean
